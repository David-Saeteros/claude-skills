# APA 7th Extended Reference Types

Reference file for the academic-writing skill. Load when the user needs a reference type not
covered by the quick-reference table in SKILL.md.

---

## Books

**Whole book (one author):**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work: Capital letter also for subtitle*. Publisher.

**Whole book (two or more authors):**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). *Title of work*. Publisher.

**Edited book:**
Editor, A. A. (Ed.). (Year). *Title of work*. Publisher.
Editor, A. A., & Editor, B. B. (Eds.). (Year). *Title of work*. Publisher.

**Chapter in edited book:**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of chapter. In A. A. Editor & B. B. Editor (Eds.),
*Title of book* (pp. xx–xx). Publisher.

**Book with edition:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of work* (Xth ed.). Publisher.

**Key change from APA 6th:** No place of publication. DOI or URL added if available.

---

## Journal Articles

**Standard (DOI available):**
Author, A. A., Author, B. B., & Author, C. C. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*, *Vol*(Issue),
pages. https://doi.org/xxxxx

**No DOI, no URL:** Omit the DOI/URL line entirely.

**Advance online publication:**
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article. *Journal Name*. Advance online publication.
https://doi.org/xxxxx

**Retracted article:**
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of article [Retracted]. *Journal Name*, *Vol*(Issue), pages.
https://doi.org/xxxxx

**21+ authors:**
List first 19 authors, then … (ellipsis), then last author.

---

## Theses and Dissertations

**Published (ProQuest / institutional repository):**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of dissertation* [Doctoral dissertation, University Name].
Repository Name. URL

**Unpublished:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of dissertation* [Unpublished doctoral dissertation]. University Name.

---

## Conference

**Paper presented (not published):**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day–Day). *Title of paper* [Paper presentation]. Conference Name,
Location.

**Conference proceedings (published):**
Author, A. A. (Year). Title of paper. In A. Editor (Ed.), *Proceedings of Conference Name*
(pp. xx–xx). Publisher. https://doi.org/xxxxx

---

## Reports and Grey Literature

**Government / institutional report:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of report* (Report No. xxx). Agency Name. URL

**Preprint:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of preprint*. Repository Name. https://doi.org/xxxxx
(Note: label as preprint if not yet peer reviewed — add "[Preprint]" after title if needed)

---

## Online Sources

**Webpage (with individual author):**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. Site Name. URL

**Webpage (organisation as author):**
Organisation Name. (Year, Month Day). *Title of page*. URL

**No date:** Use (n.d.) in place of year. In-text: (Organisation Name, n.d.)

---

## Software and Datasets

**Software:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of software* (Version x.x) [Computer software]. Publisher. URL

**R package:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *packagename: Title of package* (R package version x.x) [Computer software].
https://CRAN.R-project.org/package=packagename

**Dataset:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of dataset* (Version x) [Dataset]. Repository. https://doi.org/xxxxx

---

## Secondary Sources (Citing a Citation)

Avoid when possible — locate the original. When unavoidable:

In-text: (Original Author, Year, as cited in Secondary Author, Year)

In reference list: Include only the secondary source (the one you actually read).

## Common APA 7th mistakes to flag

| Error | Correct form |
|-------|-------------|
| "Retrieved from https://…" | Just the URL; no "Retrieved from" unless date needed |
| Place of publication included | Remove — APA 7th dropped it |
| All caps in article title | Sentence case for titles (except journals/books in italics) |
| "p." before page numbers in journals | No "p." for journals; "p." / "pp." only for book chapters |
| DOI formatted as "doi:10.xxx" | Use full hyperlink: https://doi.org/10.xxx |
| et al. in reference list | All authors listed (up to 20); never et al. in reference list |
| Ampersand in narrative citation | Use "and" in narrative: "Smith and Jones (2020)" |
| "and" in parenthetical citation | Use "&": (Smith & Jones, 2020) |
